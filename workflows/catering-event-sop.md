# Catering Event Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)
**Serving Good Vibes | Social Entertainment**
Lafayette, Louisiana

*Version 1.0 — Review annually or after major events*

---

## Purpose

This SOP ensures every catering event delivered by Serving Good Vibes reflects our standard of professional, attentive, and seamless service — from the first client inquiry through final breakdown. Every team member is responsible for upholding this standard.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **Event Coordinator** | Client communication, logistics, staffing, day-of oversight |
| **Lead Server** | Directs service staff, monitors food flow, liaises with coordinator |
| **Servers** | Food and beverage service, table setup/breakdown, guest interaction |
| **Bartenders** | Bar setup, drink service, alcohol compliance, breakdown |
| **Kitchen Lead** | Food prep, temperature control, plating, kitchen cleanliness |

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Event Planning (2+ Weeks Out)

### 1.1 Client Intake & Confirmation
- [ ] Collect completed event inquiry form (date, time, location, guest count, service type)
- [ ] Confirm dietary restrictions and allergies for all guests
- [ ] Agree on menu selections and finalize in writing
- [ ] Issue and execute signed catering agreement and deposit invoice
- [ ] Confirm event venue contact, load-in access, and parking
- [ ] Clarify client expectations: service style (buffet, plated, stations, family-style), dress code for staff, décor restrictions

### 1.2 Staffing
- [ ] Determine staffing ratio based on guest count and service style:
  - Plated/full service: 1 server per 10–15 guests
  - Buffet: 1 server per 25–30 guests
  - Bar: 1 bartender per 50 guests (minimum 2 for events over 75)
- [ ] Schedule and confirm all staff (coordinator, servers, bartenders, kitchen)
- [ ] Assign Lead Server for events with 4+ service staff
- [ ] Distribute event brief to all staff (venue, timeline, menu, dress code, client contact)

### 1.3 Sourcing & Procurement
- [ ] Place all food and beverage orders with confirmed suppliers
- [ ] Verify alcohol permits and licensing requirements for the venue
- [ ] Confirm all rental equipment (linens, chafing dishes, serving ware, glassware)
- [ ] Arrange transportation for food, equipment, and staff if needed

---

## Phase 2: Day-Before Preparation

### 2.1 Kitchen Prep
- [ ] Prepare all cold items, sauces, and pre-cooked components
- [ ] Label and date all prepped items; refrigerate at or below 40°F
- [ ] Confirm all dry goods, disposables, and supplies are packed and staged
- [ ] Test all equipment (chafers, warming trays, portable burners)

### 2.2 Logistics Pack-Out Checklist
- [ ] Serving utensils (tongs, spoons, ladles — one per item)
- [ ] Chafing dishes and fuel
- [ ] Linens (table, buffet, bar)
- [ ] Glassware, plates, flatware (plus 15% overage)
- [ ] Bar supplies (ice, openers, pourers, straws, cocktail napkins, garnishes)
- [ ] Sanitation supplies (gloves, sanitizer, food-safe wipes, trash bags)
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Client folder (signed contract, run-of-show, floor plan, staff list)

### 2.3 Staff Confirmation
- [ ] Send day-before reminder to all staff with call time, address, parking, and dress code
- [ ] Confirm backup contacts in case of staff no-shows

---

## Phase 3: Day-of Execution

### 3.1 Load-In & Setup

**Coordinator arrives first — minimum 2.5 hours before service start.**

- [ ] Check in with venue contact; confirm access to kitchen, bar area, and event space
- [ ] Walk the space and confirm layout matches agreed floor plan
- [ ] Direct staff on equipment placement and table setup
- [ ] Set up buffet/stations or table settings per service style
- [ ] Set up bar (ice, bottles, garnishes, glassware organized by type)
- [ ] Confirm all food is held at correct temperature before service:
  - Hot food: 135°F or above
  - Cold food: 40°F or below

### 3.2 Staff Briefing (30 Minutes Before Service)
Coordinator conducts a mandatory pre-service huddle covering:
- [ ] Event timeline and key moments (guest arrival, meal service, toasts, cake cutting, etc.)
- [ ] Menu walkthrough — ingredients, allergens, descriptions staff should know
- [ ] Guest experience expectations — greet warmly, anticipate needs, no phones during service
- [ ] Emergency protocols — nearest exits, first aid kit location, coordinator as point of contact
- [ ] Signal system for refills, kitchen needs, and wrap-up cues

### 3.3 Service Execution

**Guest Arrival**
- [ ] Servers stationed and ready 10 minutes before guests arrive
- [ ] Welcome drinks or passed appetizers deployed on schedule
- [ ] Bartenders open bar per agreed start time

**During Service**
- [ ] Monitor buffet/station levels — replenish before items run below one-third
- [ ] Clear plates and glassware continuously; never let tables become cluttered
- [ ] Check in with lead server every 30 minutes on food levels and guest feedback
- [ ] Coordinator checks in with client contact at arrival, mid-event, and before breakdown
- [ ] Log any incidents (allergic reactions, spills, complaints) in real time

**Bar Service**
- [ ] Never serve visibly intoxicated guests — coordinator has final authority on cut-offs
- [ ] Check ID for guests who appear under 30
- [ ] Keep bar area clean and stocked throughout event
- [ ] Track consumption for post-event client reporting if requested

### 3.4 End of Service
- [ ] Coordinate with client on exact service close time (last call for bar, last food pass)
- [ ] Thank guests as they transition out of service
- [ ] Begin clearing when coordinator signals — not before client confirms

---

## Phase 4: Breakdown & Closeout

### 4.1 Breakdown
- [ ] Remove all food from service immediately after close; discard per food safety guidelines
- [ ] Pack all SGV equipment separately from venue property
- [ ] Strip linens; bag for laundry
- [ ] Clean and sanitize all surfaces touched during event (bar, buffet tables, kitchen prep areas)
- [ ] Dispose of all trash; leave space in equal or better condition than found
- [ ] Conduct walkthrough with venue contact before leaving

### 4.2 Leftover Food Policy
- [ ] Offer packaged leftovers to client only if food has been held within safe temperature range throughout service
- [ ] Do not leave perishables behind unless client has proper storage
- [ ] Document any food donated per food safety compliance

### 4.3 Equipment Return
- [ ] Inventory all SGV equipment against pack-out list before loading
- [ ] Flag any damaged or missing items immediately; document with photos
- [ ] Return rentals to vendor within agreed window

---

## Phase 5: Post-Event Follow-Up (Within 48 Hours)

### 5.1 Client Follow-Up
- [ ] Send thank-you message to client within 24 hours
- [ ] Deliver final invoice if balance remains outstanding
- [ ] Request testimonial or review if event went well
- [ ] Log any feedback (positive or corrective) for future planning

### 5.2 Internal Debrief
- [ ] Coordinator submits post-event notes covering:
  - Actual guest count vs. estimated
  - Food quantities used vs. ordered (waste or shortage notes)
  - Staff performance notes
  - Any incidents or complaints
  - What worked well / what to improve
- [ ] Update client file with debrief notes
- [ ] Adjust future staffing ratios or menu quantities based on learnings

### 5.3 Financial Closeout
- [ ] Confirm all staff hours logged and approved
- [ ] Reconcile food and supply costs against event budget
- [ ] Calculate event profitability (revenue − food cost − labor − rentals − transport)
- [ ] File completed event record in financial tracker

## Food Safety Standards

All SGV catering staff must adhere to the following at all times:

- **Temperature Danger Zone:** Never hold food between 40°F and 135°F for more than 2 hours
- **Handwashing:** Before service, after handling raw food, after breaks, and after any contact with contaminants
- **Gloves:** Required when handling ready-to-eat food; change between food types
- **Cross-contamination:** Keep raw proteins separate from all other food; use color-coded cutting boards
- **Labeling:** All prepped food must be labeled with item name and prep date
- **ServSafe:** All kitchen leads and bartenders must maintain current ServSafe certification

## Service Standards

- Greet every guest with eye contact and a warm welcome
- Anticipate needs — refill before asked, clear before it's cluttered
- If you don't know the answer to a guest question, find someone who does — never guess about allergens
- No personal phone use during service — coordinator only during emergencies
- Dress code is non-negotiable; arrive event-ready
- Represent SGV and Social Entertainment at all times — on and off the clock at the venue

## Emergency Contacts & Escalation

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Guest allergic reaction | Call 911 immediately; notify coordinator; document incident |
| Intoxicated guest incident | Coordinator manages; contact venue security if needed |
| Food safety concern (temperature, contamination) | Remove item from service immediately; notify coordinator; do not serve |
| Staff no-show day-of | Coordinator activates backup staff list |
| Client complaint | Coordinator handles directly; do not argue; document and escalate to management |
